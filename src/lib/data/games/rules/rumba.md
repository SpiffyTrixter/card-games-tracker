# **Rumba Jass: Official Rules**

## **1. Objective**

Start with **15 points**. Be the first player to reach **0 or below**.

- If multiple players reach $\le 0$ in the same round, the player with the **lowest (most negative)** score wins.

## **2. Setup**

- **Deck:** Standard 36-card Jass deck.
- **Rank:** Ace (High), King, Queen, Jack, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6 (Low). **Note:** The Jack and 9 have no special value in this game.
- **Deal:** Each player receives **5 cards**.
- **Trump:** Flip the next card from the deck; its suit is **Trump** for the round.
- **Rotation:** The Dealer role and the starting turn move one player to the **right** every round.

---

## **3. The Pre-Play Phases**

Before the first card is played, players must complete these three phases in order, starting with the player to the right of the dealer.

### **Phase 1: The Commitment**

Each player declares if they are **"In"** or **"Out."**

- **Sit Out:** You fold your hand and receive **0 points**.
- _Constraint 1:_ You cannot sit out if your current score is **5 or lower**.
- _Constraint 2:_ You cannot sit out more than **2 times in a row**.

- **Play:** You proceed to Phase 2.

### **Phase 2: Calling Rumba**

Active players decide if they want to call **Rumba**. Multiple players can call Rumba in the same round.

- **The Goal:** You must win **at least 3 tricks**.
- **The Reward:** All points gained or lost are **doubled**.
- **Phase 3 Skip:** If **any** player calls Rumba, Phase 3 (Improvement) is **skipped**. Players must play with their original cards.

### **Phase 3: Card Improvement (The Exchange)**

_This phase only occurs if **no one** called Rumba._

- Each active player can discard up to **4 cards** and draw replacements from the deck.
- The exchange happens in turn order.
- If the deck runs out, the very last player to draw will receive the face-up **Trump Card** from the table as their final replacement.

---

## **4. Gameplay**

- **Lead:** The active player to the right of the dealer leads the first trick.
- **Following Suit:** You **must** follow the suit led if you are able.
- **Trumping:** You must play a Trump card if you **cannot** follow the led suit.
- **Winning:** The highest card of the led suit wins, unless a Trump is played. The highest Trump wins the trick. The winner of the trick leads the next one.
- **Lone Player:** If only one player stays "In," they play alone to maximize their score (they can even call Rumba to get -10 points).

---

## **5. Scoring**

| Action          | Result                | Points               |
| --------------- | --------------------- | -------------------- |
| **Normal Play** | Win 1 or more tricks  | **-1** per trick won |
| **Normal Play** | Win 0 tricks          | **+5**               |
| **Rumba**       | Win 3, 4, or 5 tricks | **-2** per trick won |
| **Rumba**       | Win 0, 1, or 2 tricks | **+10**              |
| **Sit Out**     | Folded hand           | **0**                |

---

### **Summary Table for Scores**

- **3 Tricks (Normal):** -3 pts
- **3 Tricks (Rumba):** -6 pts
- **5 Tricks (Rumba):** -10 pts
- **Fail Rumba:** +10 pts
- **0 Tricks (Normal):** +5 pts
